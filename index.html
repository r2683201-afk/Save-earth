About Nature

The Living World We Must Protect

Nature is the foundation of all life. Forests breathe, rivers flow, mountains stand — and together they sustain every living being on Earth.

Forests

Forests cover 31% of the Earth and produce the oxygen we breathe. They are home to 80% of terrestrial biodiversity.

Rivers & Oceans

Freshwater sustains civilizations. Oceans regulate climate, feed billions, and produce more than half the world's oxygen.

Wildlife

Every species plays a role in the ecosystem. Protecting wildlife means protecting the delicate balance of life itself.

Critical Issues

Problems Facing Nature Today

Human activity has pushed ecosystems to the brink. Understanding these challenges is the first step toward solving them.

Deforestation

10 million hectares of forest lost each year.

Plastic Pollution

11 million tons of plastic enter oceans annually.

Air Pollution

9 out of 10 people breathe contaminated air.

Climate Change

Global temperatures rose 1.2°C since pre-industrial times.

Water Scarcity

2 billion people lack safe drinking water access.

Take Action

Daily Eco-Friendly Habits

Small changes, massive impact. Start today with these simple habits that protect our planet every single day.

Carry Reusable Bags

Skip plastic bags and use cloth ones every shopping trip.

Drink Tap Water

Use a refillable bottle and cut out single-use plastic.

Use Solar Power

Switch to solar lamps and renewable energy wherever possible.

Compost Food Waste

Turn scraps into rich soil instead of sending them to landfills.

Conserve Electricity

Turn off unused lights and unplug chargers when not in use.

Walk or Cycle

Choose human-powered transport for short trips to reduce emissions.

Support Local

Buy local, seasonal produce to cut food miles and support farmers.

Spread Awareness

Share knowledge and inspire others to join the green movement.
